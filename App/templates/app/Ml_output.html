Normal Fetal Condition

The fetal condition is considered normal when there are no signs of distress or abnormalities in the monitoring results.

Symptoms

Symptoms
Stable heart rate
Regular fetal movements
Normal amniotic fluid levels

Prevention & Precautions

Maintain a healthy lifestyle, balanced diet, and regular prenatal checkups.

Recommended Physical Activities

Gentle exercises like walking, swimming, and prenatal yoga.

Diet Recommendations

Fruits Vegetables Vitamins
Apples, Bananas, Oranges Spinach, Carrots, Broccoli Folic acid, Vitamin D, Omega-3

Baby Activities

Encourage gentle movements and play soothing music to promote relaxation.

{% elif predict == 'Suspicious' %}

Suspicious Fetal Condition

There are some irregularities observed that may need further monitoring or assessment.

Symptoms

Symptoms
Irregular heart rate
Reduced fetal movements
Unusual levels of amniotic fluid

Prevention & Precautions

Ensure close monitoring and follow your doctor's advice for additional tests.

Recommended Physical Activities

Low-impact exercises such as stretching and slow walking.

Diet Recommendations

Fruits Vegetables Vitamins
Berries, Pears, Kiwis Kale, Bell Peppers, Sweet Potatoes Iron, Calcium, Vitamin B12

Potential Effects on Other Conditions

Suspicious results may indicate a risk of conditions like gestational diabetes or preeclampsia.

Baby Activities

Engage in soft touch and gentle movements to stimulate fetal activity.

{% elif predict == 'Pathological' %}

Pathological Fetal Condition

This indicates a high-risk condition that requires immediate medical intervention.

Symptoms

Symptoms
Severe heart rate irregularities
Minimal or absent fetal movements
Abnormal amniotic fluid levels

Prevention & Precautions

Seek emergency care, follow high-risk pregnancy protocols, and ensure frequent monitoring.

Recommended Physical Activities

Only under doctor's supervision; rest is often recommended.

Diet Recommendations

Fruits Vegetables Vitamins
Pomegranate, Avocado, Mango Beets, Asparagus, Seaweed Vitamin E, Magnesium, Zinc

Potential Effects on Other Conditions

Pathological conditions may affect other organs and could be related to complications like fetal growth restriction or maternal hypertension.

Baby Activities

Minimal stimulation is recommended; prioritize monitoring and medical advice.
